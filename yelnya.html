Yelnya Offensive

Date

30 Aug – 8 Sep 1941

Location

Yelnya, USSR

Result

Soviet Victory

Overview

An early successful Soviet counterattack during Operation Barbarossa that regained ground and created the first Soviet 'Guards' units.

Faction Commanders Casualties
USSR Zhukov ~31,000 casualties
Germany Von Bock ~45,000 casualties

The Conflict

Soviet forces eliminated a German bridgehead threatening Moscow, using concentrated attacks to dislodge defenders and consolidate positions.

The victory had propaganda value and provided lessons for future Soviet offensives; Guards units were formed to recognize exceptional performance.

Significance & Aftermath

  • First successful Soviet offensive action reclaiming territory.
  • Boosted morale and led to formation of elite 'Guards' units.
