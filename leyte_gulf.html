Battle of Leyte Gulf

Date

23–26 October 1944

Location

Leyte Gulf, Philippines

Result

Decisive Allied Victory

Overview

The largest naval battle in history, involving multiple separate engagements that destroyed much of the Imperial Japanese Navy.

Faction Commanders Casualties
Allied (US, Australia) Halsey, Kinkaid ~3,000 killed, 6 ships lost
Japan Kurita, Ozawa ~12,500 killed, 26 ships lost

The Conflict

Comprised four main actions (Sibuyan Sea, Surigao Strait, Samar, and Cape Engaño). The battle saw the first large-scale use of kamikaze attacks and the near-total destruction of Japan's remaining naval strength.

Although parts of the Japanese plan nearly succeeded tactically, Allied air power and radar-directed gunnery, as well as superior logistics, ensured their defeat.

Significance & Aftermath

  • Crippled the Imperial Japanese Navy's ability to operate as a major fleet.
  • Secured the liberation of the Philippines and improved Allied control of sea lanes.
