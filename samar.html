Battle off Samar

Date

25 October 1944

Location

Off Samar, Philippine Sea

Result

American Victory (Japanese withdrew)

Overview

A small US escort carrier force and destroyers (Taffy 3) heroically defended against a much larger Japanese surface force.

Faction Commanders Casualties
USA (Taffy 3) Clifton Sprague ~1,000 killed
Japan Takeo Kurita 3 heavy cruisers sunk (and others damaged)

The Conflict

Despite being outgunned, US escorts used smoke, torpedoes, and aggressive tactics to confuse and damage Japanese ships; the appearance of more American ships and aggressive counterattacks led Kurita to withdraw.

The action showcased extraordinary bravery and contributed directly to protecting the Leyte landing forces from annihilation.

Significance & Aftermath

  • Prevented a potential catastrophe for the Leyte landings.
  • Demonstrated that small, determined forces could disrupt larger fleets through tactics and bold leadership.
