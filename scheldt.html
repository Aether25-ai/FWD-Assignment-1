Battle of the Scheldt

Date

Oct 2 – Nov 8, 1944

Location

Northern Belgium / SW Netherlands

Result

Allied Victory

Overview

A miserable, muddy campaign led by the First Canadian Army to open the shipping route to the port of Antwerp.

Faction Commanders Casualties
Allies (Canada/UK) Guy Simonds ~12,873 casualties
Germany Gustav-Adolf von Zangen ~10,000-12,000 killed

The Conflict

Fought in flooded polders and dykes. It was amphibious warfare in mud. The terrain offered zero cover, making it a "soldier's nightmare."

Significance & Aftermath

  • Opening Antwerp was critical for supplying the Allied advance into Germany.
  • Without this port, the advance would have stalled.
